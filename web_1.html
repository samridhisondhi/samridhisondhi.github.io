Recent Developments in Electronics

Mentions: Avionics, Motorsport, Technology, Marine & Defence and Design Engineering

Interactive Flight Management system [i-FMS].

Universal Avionics completed initial flight testing of its software, goal is to enable NextGen capabilities using global navigation systems.

Human Machine Interface/Augumented Reality.

Augmented reality (AR) is an overlay of digital information onto the real world. In the case of automotive electronics, AR uses a dashboard-mounted camera to obtain images of the road ahead, then projects digital Information

Advanced Driver-Assistance systems

Adaptive cruise control, an advanced driver-assistance system that can automatically adjust speed to make sure a car maintains a safe distance behind another vehicle.

Integration of AIS Technology

Automatic Identification System (AIS) technology is becoming increasingly integrated into marine electronics devices, providing vessels with real-time identification and tracking capabilities. It allows ships to exchange information.

Additive Manufacturing

Increased use of 3D printing in defense for producing equipment and spare parts on-demand, reducing dependence on traditional supply chains.

Low-Code and No-Code AI

Artificial Intelligence has shifted its gears from technical lingo to user-friendly interface with low-code and no-code solutions. The ability to build solutions without the expertise of coding or technical know-how has made it the top pick and successful among the business leaders.
